The Final Game

it’s played in a court shaped like a squid. The rules are simple. Players are divided into two groups, the offense and the defense. Once the game starts, the defense can run around on two feet in bounds, while the offense outside the lines are only allowed to hop on one foot. But if an attacker cuts through the waist of the squid past the defense, they are given the freedom to use both feet. called that ‘inspector royale.’ When everyone ready for the final battle, the attackers gathered at the entrance to the squid.

In order to win, the attackers must tap the small closed off space on the squid’s head with their foot. But if someone on the defense manages to push attacker outside the squid’s boundary, attacker will die. Once attacker tap the squid’s head, attacker win and yell out, ‘Victory.

Why 456 Players?

The Strategy Behind Game Nepal's Players Count

In order to recreate the authentic experience of the Squid Game series and ensure the integrity of the competition, we aim to have 456 Playerss. This specific number not only pays homage to the show but also allows for a diverse and dynamic pool of Players, enhancing the excitement and unpredictability of the games. Additionally, with a larger number of Players we can showcase a wide range of talents and skills, making the competition even more engaging for both Players and viewers alike.
